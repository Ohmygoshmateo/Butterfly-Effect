What this prototype is

The Butterfly Effect is a fully functional decision path modeling application. The user describes a real decision in plain language, optionally adds personal context, and receives a best case, most likely case, and worst case projection for the near term, the mid term, and the long term, plus the secondary areas of life the decision tends to affect.

How outcomes are generated

The prototype works in three steps. First, a keyword classifier reads the decision text and identifies which of eight life domains it belongs to (career, military, business, education, finance, health, relationships, or location). Second, it retrieves outcome narratives for that domain from a built-in library of 216 hand-written variants, selecting different versions on every run. Third, it personalizes the most likely path using any age or situation details the user provides. No artificial intelligence service or external data source is involved at runtime.

How it is built

The application is written in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with no frameworks and no external dependencies. It runs entirely in the browser, requires no installation or account, works offline once loaded, and stores nothing the user types. It was designed and built as the final capstone prototype for ITCC298.

Is this actually free?

Yes. The full working prototype is free, with no account and no ads. The premium tier is a planned future upgrade, not a gate on anything you see today.

Does it give me advice?

No, by design. It shows you a realistic best case, most likely case, and worst case so you can decide with clear eyes. The decision is always yours.

User Guide

Everything you need to know, in plain language. No experience required.

1. Getting started

There is nothing to install and no account to create. The application runs right here in your browser and works even without an internet connection once the page is open. Head to the page whenever you are ready.

2. Describe your decision

Type the decision you are considering into the large box, the same way you would say it to a friend. For example: "Quit my job to go back to school." If you are not sure what to write, click one of the example buttons and the box fills itself in.

3. Make it yours (optional)

Visit the page to save your age, situation, and priorities. The prototype uses them automatically. Or click "Add your situation" inside the prototype to enter them there. Either way, nothing is stored after you close the page.

4. Read your three paths

Click "Show me my paths." For each time period you get a best case (green, up arrow), a most likely case (gold, right arrow), and a worst case (red, down arrow). Below them, "What else this affects" points out other parts of your life the decision touches, like money, family, or health.

5. Explore the views

Timeline shows your outcomes in order over time. Snapshot compares all time periods side by side for one path type at a time. Use the Copy button to save your results, or Re-run to see the same paths with fresh wording. You cannot break anything by clicking around.

A few important reminders

The paths are realistic estimates based on how similar decisions tend to unfold. They are not predictions and not advice. For decisions involving serious money, legal, medical, or emotional matters, use this as a starting point and then talk with a qualified professional. The final call is always yours.
